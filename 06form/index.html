Fun Facts

Not all Bees Produce Honey.

Less than 5 percent of bee species make honey. Only honey bees and bees that don't sting produce enough honey to make it worth harvesting.

Stingless bees

  • Stingless bees are usually found in tropicals areas.
  • Stingless bees usually only polinate the area where they are found.
  • Stinglees bee honey is pretty rare, they tipacally only make enough to sustain their hive. So when it's sold it's in small quantities.

Bees and Their roles

  • The three main roles for honey bees are drones, workers, and the queen.
  • Drones are the male bees, they mate with the queen until the winter when they are kicked out.
  • Worker bees are the bees that we usually see, they're the pollinators and they build and protect their hive.
  • The queen runs the hive and produces bees to keep the hive alive.
  • Worker bees usually only live 5-6 weeks, whereas the queen can live up to 5 years.
